Leclerc Leads Ferrari 2-3 at Silverstone as Antonelli Fades

Charles Leclerc claimed his second victory of the 2026 season at the British Grand Prix, leading a Ferrari 2-3 finish at Silverstone in one of the tightest races of the year. Starting from second on the grid, Leclerc crossed the line in 1:27:11.335 after 52 laps, surviving relentless pressure from behind on a day when the top four finishers were covered by just 1.149 seconds.

George Russell finished second for Mercedes, a mere 0.427 seconds behind the Ferrari — a heartbreaking near-miss for the British driver at his home Grand Prix. Lewis Hamilton completed the podium in third, only 0.772 seconds from the win, giving Ferrari its first double podium of the season at the team's 2026 recruit's home circuit. Lando Norris took fourth for McLaren, just 1.149 seconds off the lead, rounding out a front group that ran nose-to-tail for much of the afternoon.

Isack Hadjar impressed with fifth place for Red Bull Racing at 1.598 seconds, continuing his consistent rookie campaign. The Racing Bulls duo of Liam Lawson and Arvid Lindblad followed in sixth and seventh, separated by less than two tenths at 2.023 and 2.214 seconds respectively. Gabriel Bortoleto scored four valuable points for Audi in eighth, while Franco Colapinto delivered a standout drive from nineteenth on the grid to ninth — gaining ten positions — with Alpine teammate Pierre Gasly completing the top ten.

The race's biggest shock was pole-sitter Andrea Kimi Antonelli, who fell to sixteenth place after a difficult afternoon that saw the championship leader finish 8.005 seconds off the pace. Oscar Piastri could only manage eleventh for McLaren after starting eighth, while Max Verstappen's difficult season continued — the Red Bull driver retired after 46 laps without a classified finish.

Three drivers failed to finish. Max Verstappen exited after 46 laps, Alexander Albon retired his Williams after 43 laps, and Nico Hülkenberg's Audi stopped after 36 laps.

In the championship, Antonelli's lead remains intact at 179 points despite his scoreless afternoon, but Russell has closed the gap to 25 points with 154. Hamilton sits third on 147, with Leclerc jumping to fourth on 108 after his victory — now just 39 points behind his Ferrari teammate. Norris rounds out the top five on 97. In the constructor standings, Mercedes still leads with 333 points, but Ferrari's 1-3 result cuts the deficit — Ferrari now has 255, 78 points behind. McLaren holds third with 179.

The championship heads to Spa-Francorchamps for the Belgian Grand Prix on July 19, where the legendary Ardennes circuit awaits with its unpredictable weather and the fearsome Eau Rouge-Raidillon complex.
