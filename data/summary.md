# Spanish Grand Prix — Race Summary

**Lewis Hamilton** claimed his first victory for Ferrari in commanding fashion at the Circuit de Barcelona-Catalunya, leading a strategic masterclass from the front row to win by nearly 20 seconds.

## Key Moments

- **Lap 1:** Hamilton jumped polesitter George Russell off the line and immediately built a gap. Behind, chaos as Lance Stroll collided with the barriers at Turn 2 — race over on lap 5.
- **Lap 15:** Valtteri Bottas pulled off with a mechanical failure, the first of several retirements on a scorching Spanish afternoon.
- **Lap 30:** Disaster for Nico Hulkenberg — Audi's home-race hopes ended with a power unit failure.
- **Lap 38:** Fernando Alonso, racing in front of his home crowd, retired with a suspected gearbox issue — a heartbreaking end for the Aston Martin veteran.
- **Lap 55–62:** The attrition continued. Championship leader **Andrea Kimi Antonelli** suffered a late-race failure from P3 and was forced to park his Mercedes. Teammate **Charles Leclerc** followed shortly after, capping a disastrous day for Ferrari's rivals — but Hamilton's win more than compensated for the Scuderia.

## The Finishers

Hamilton crossed the line at **1:32:28.105** with Russell 19 seconds back in second. Lando Norris completed the podium for McLaren, while Max Verstappen (P4) and Oscar Piastri (P5) rounded out the top five. Isack Hadjar took P6 for Red Bull Racing — the team's best finish of the weekend — with Pierre Gasly collecting solid points for Alpine in P7.

## Lapped Traffic

The midfield battle was fierce: Liam Lawson (P8), Arvid Lindblad (P9), and Franco Colapinto (P10) all finished one lap down but within 55 seconds of each other across the line.

## Championship Picture

Despite his DNF, **Antonelli holds the championship lead** with 156 points — but Hamilton's victory tightens the gap to just 41 points. Mercedes (262pts) remains firmly in control of the constructors' race over Ferrari (190pts).

*Next up: the Austrian Grand Prix at the Red Bull Ring, June 28 — a home race for Red Bull and a chance for Verstappen to turn his season around.*
