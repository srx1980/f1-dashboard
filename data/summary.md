# Belgian Grand Prix 2026 — Antonelli wins at Spa

Andrea Kimi Antonelli took victory at the Belgian Grand Prix, the 10th round of the 2026 Formula 1 season, starting from pole at the Circuit de Spa-Francorchamps and converting it into his sixth win of the year. The Mercedes driver completed 44 laps in 1:24:42.479, finishing 1.952 seconds clear of Charles Leclerc in the Ferrari. Max Verstappen completed the podium for Red Bull Racing, 11.586 seconds behind the winner.

Lewis Hamilton finished fourth in the second Ferrari, 17.245 seconds back, with Oscar Piastri fifth for McLaren, 18.988 seconds off the lead. Isack Hadjar was an impressive sixth for Red Bull Racing, having started 21st on the grid, ahead of Lando Norris in the McLaren. Gabriel Bortoleto (Audi) and Arvid Lindblad (Racing Bulls) rounded out the top eight. The midfield was covered by Franco Colapinto, Pierre Gasly, Liam Lawson and Nico Hulkenberg, all finishing on the same lap within a 90-second window of the winner.

Three drivers failed to reach the finish. George Russell retired on the opening lap, while Sergio Perez exited on lap 13 and Lance Stroll on lap 25. Valtteri Bottas and Fernando Alonso were classified 18th and 19th, one and two laps down respectively.

Antonelli's victory extends his championship lead to 204 points, 45 clear of Lewis Hamilton on 159, with George Russell third on 154. Charles Leclerc's runner-up finish moves him to 126 points in fourth, ahead of Lando Norris on 103. In the constructors' standings, Mercedes holds a commanding lead with 358 points, 73 ahead of Ferrari on 285, with McLaren third on 195.

The championship moves to the Hungaroring for the Hungarian Grand Prix on July 26.
