# George Russell Wins Austrian Grand Prix in Mercedes 1-3

George Russell claimed his second victory of the 2026 season at the Red Bull Ring, leading a Mercedes 1-3 finish in a race defined by tight margins at the front. Russell crossed the line in 1:26:39.011 after 71 laps of the Spielberg circuit, surviving intense pressure from behind.

Max Verstappen finished second for Red Bull Racing, just 1.641 seconds adrift of the Mercedes — a strong recovery drive from fifth on the grid that saw him push Russell all the way to the checkered flag. Andrea Kimi Antonelli completed the podium in his Mercedes, only 2.087 seconds behind his teammate, extending his remarkable rookie championship lead.

Oscar Piastri took fourth for McLaren, 21.909 seconds off the lead, with Lewis Hamilton crossing the line fifth for Ferrari at 26.895 seconds. Isack Hadjar impressed with sixth place for Red Bull Racing, while Lando Norris and Charles Leclerc rounded out the top eight in a tightly packed midfield. Liam Lawson and Arvid Lindblad both finished one lap down to complete the points-paying positions for Racing Bulls.

Gabriel Bortoleto, Nico Hulkenberg, Pierre Gasly, Oliver Bearman, and Franco Colapinto all finished one lap down, while Esteban Ocon and Alexander Albon were two laps behind. Fernando Alonso brought his Aston Martin home three laps in arrears.

Four drivers failed to finish. Lance Stroll retired after 45 laps, while Carlos Sainz exited after 23. Sergio Perez lasted only four laps before retiring, and Valtteri Bottas recorded the earliest exit — his Cadillac stopped after just two laps.

In the championship, Antonelli remains firmly in control with 171 points. Russell's victory moves him into second with 131, overtaking Hamilton who now sits third on 125. Piastri climbs to fourth on 80 points, while Leclerc and Norris are tied at 79. Mercedes extends its constructor lead to 302 points, 98 clear of Ferrari on 204. McLaren holds third with 159.

The championship now heads to Silverstone for the British Grand Prix on July 5.
